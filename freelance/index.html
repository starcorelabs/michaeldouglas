Client Meeting

During my first meeting with a potential client, my goal is to learn about their project, challenges, and goals. This will help me determine if I'm the right designer for their project.

Here are some of the questions I ask:

  • Have you shopped around for other designers?
  • Who is the target audience and what problem are we solving for them?
  • What research and resource gathering have you done?
  • What is the timeframe of the project?

My Contract

Once I agree to take on a client we review my contract and clarify the timeframe, scope of service, deliverables, and pricing. After the contract is signed and the down payment is made, I get started working. Freelance Design Contract  

Design Process

I use the industry standard Design Thinking process to research and create deliverables.

  1. Empathize - Research User Needs
  2. Define - State Users's Needs and Problems
  3. Ideate - Challenge Assumptions and Create Ideas
  4. Prototype - Start to Create Solutions
  5. Test - Try the Solution Out
  6. Implement - Provide the Solution to Stakeholders.

Note: I do not store client files after project completion.

Pricing

I require a base cost of $1000.00 to take on new projects.
From there, clients can choose one of three pricing models.

  1. Hourly
  2. Project-Based
  3. Value-Based

Hourly

This model seems to work best for businesses contracting out for short-term design projects. I make it clear to clients that this is the most expensive pricing model I offer. Some businesses still choose it due to internal bureaucracy. My hourly rate is $60.00 and must be paid weekly.

Project-Based

This model works best for small UX/UI projects or simple graphical deliverables. This is a flat fee for the design work I do on the project. It must be fully paid in advance before I start working. The total price is determined by the complexity, effort, and time it takes to create the deliverables.

Value-Based

This model is best for medium to large projects. Each project is unique and so is it's value to you and your customers.

I start with the projected revenue this project will generate for your company and then estimate 30%. Then I add on the complexity, effort, and time it takes to create those deliverables.  

Payments

  • Hourly-based pricing must be paid weekly.
  • Project-based pricing is paid in full before I start work on the project.
  • Value-based project payments are split up into two parts. Half upfront and half upon completion of the project.

After I receive the full agreed-upon payment. Then the deliverables are zipped and emailed to the client.
